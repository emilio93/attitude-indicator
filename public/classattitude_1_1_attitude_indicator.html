Detailed Description

The AttitudeIndicator is the full representation of the attitude indicator.

The state of the indicator consists of a point along the center of the screen and a degree of inclination.

The state is determined by a Y position that indicates a point along a vertical line in the center of the screen, an M inclination and a inclination direction(sign of M).

For a screen of (Xmax, Ymax) pixels.

y(x) = M * x + b(Y)

So we want to obtain b from Y:

y(Xmax/2) = Y = M * Xmax/2 + b b = Y - M * Xmax/2

And finally redifine for y(x)

y(x) = M * x + Y - M * Xmax/2 y(x) = M * (x - Xmax/2) + Y

This is the equation for the horizon line.

y(x) = M * (x - Xmax/2) + Y

Noting the Y axis is inverted in the screen.

y(x) = M * (Xmax/2 - x) - Y
